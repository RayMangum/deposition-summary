Overall Summary

This deposition volume (Volume II) continues the examination of Joseph Nadeau regarding his employment as a laborer at the Centredale site, operated by Metro-Atlantic Chemical Company and/or New England Container Company (NECC), during the early to mid-1960s. Having been previously deposed (in 2002 and 2008) and having testified at trial (in 2006), much of this session involved reviewing and confirming or clarifying that prior testimony, often prompted by examiners presenting transcripts or exhibits.

A recurring theme is Mr. Nadeau's admittedly "fuzzy" or "foggy" memory of events occurring approximately 50 years prior. He often deferred to his prior, closer-in-time testimony (particularly from 2002 and 2006) as likely being more accurate than his current independent recollection. However, he did provide present-day descriptions and clarifications, especially when guided by a site map marked during his 2008 deposition (Exhibit 8).

Key areas explored include:

  • Timeline of Employment: Worked part-time summer jobs (~1962-1965) while in high school. Believes NECC work was ~'62/'63, Metro-Atlantic primarily '64-'65, ending August 1965 upon military draft.
  • Site Operations & Layout: Used a site map (Exhibit 8) to identify and describe functions of: office, process areas, shipping, outdoor storage tanks, indoor filter presses (distinct "Reserve Salt" and "40-S"), separate maintenance/salt drying building, the "Texas Tower" (associated with hexachlorophene), and extensive drum handling areas (loading, warehouse, paint station, burn pit/furnace, wash station, outdoor storage, "dump" area).
  • Waste Handling & Discharges: Described basement French drains for washdowns. Reaffirmed prior testimony that solid wastes (filter cake, chipped material) likely went into an on-site Dumpster with empty bags. Confirmed prior testimony about removing plastic liners from drums at NECC, which were temporarily discarded on the ground, collected, and potentially disposed of in the dump area. Consistently maintained (based on prior testimony and current recall) that basement wash water via French drains went "outside" towards the tailrace/river, not a sewer. Clarified that while initially calling his observation of the discharge point an "assumption" in 2002, he now recalls actually seeing the discharge exit the building. Confirmed observing the tailrace become murky at times.
  • Hexachlorophene: Stood by prior testimony estimating hexachlorophene manufacture in the "Texas Tower" for ~8-9 months before he left in August 1965.
  • Relationship Between Companies: Perceived Metro-Atlantic and NECC as interconnected operations under related families (Buonannos), sharing resources like "interchangeable" maintenance staff.
  • Knowledge Limitations: Emphasized role as a laborer, lack of chemical knowledge (didn't recognize "dioxin" term), and no knowledge of corporate structure, accounting, or property lines.

Overall, the deposition aimed to solidify Mr. Nadeau's recollections, often via confirmation of prior sworn testimony, regarding operational practices, site layout, and waste handling. The testimony highlighted both the specific details he could recall or confirm and the admitted limitations of his memory and knowledge after five decades. Frequent objections were raised by counsel regarding vagueness, leading questions, foundation, and the use of prior testimony.

Detailed Summary

Examination by Mr. Pirozzolo (Emhart)

Examiner's Objective & Success Assessment

Mr. Pirozzolo aimed to establish the timeline of Mr. Nadeau's employment, confirm details from his prior deposition (Exhibit 1) and trial testimony regarding specific operations (hexachlorophene production, waste handling), clarify the physical layout and processes using a site map (Exhibit 8), and probe his observations of discharges from the facility's French drains. Mr. Pirozzolo was largely successful in having Mr. Nadeau confirm his prior testimony on key points and effectively used the site map (Exhibit 8) to elicit detailed descriptions of operational areas. However, the witness frequently qualified his answers due to the passage of time ("fuzzy," "foggy memory," "guessing"), sometimes relying more on the presented prior testimony than independent recall. Mr. Pirozzolo also highlighted a potential evolution in the witness's certainty regarding whether he directly *saw* discharges versus assuming they occurred.

Employment Dates & Duration

  • Worked summer/part-time jobs between '62 and '65 while in Ponaganset High School (age 16/17 range).(p. 151)
  • Believes NECC work was one summer ('62 or '63); Metro-Atlantic primarily '64 and '65.(p. 152)
    A. I think the last two stints were at Metro-Atlantic. I think '64 through '65 was at Metro for sure. '63, '64 may have also been with Metro-Atlantic. Not too sure of that, and then '62 I believe was -- '62, '63 was the New England Container.(p. 152)
  • Last day working at Centredale site was around August 1965, before military draft.(p. 153)

Hexachlorophene Production

  • Recalled the "Texas Tower" building constructed during a summer he wasn't there.(p. 153)
  • Confirmed prior deposition testimony (Exhibit 1, p. 59, Dec 17, 2002) estimating hexachlorophene manufacture for ~8-9 months before he left.(pp. 154, 156)
    Q. It would be your testimony that on December 17, 2002 you testified truthfully to the best of your recollection? A. Yes, I did.(p. 156)
    (Reading prior testimony) "Answer: I was there about a year full time. I'm guessing maybe eight, nine months maybe. It wasn't there full time. I can't remember. I know it was there for a good eight months anyhow."(p. 156)
  • Stated memory was better in 2002 but stands by truthfulness of prior testimony.(p. 156)

Main Building Details (Floors, Drains, Windows, Slope)

  • Main building: 3-4 floors, including basement.(p. 157)
  • French drains located in basement level.(p. 157)
  • Land sloped slightly; parking lot uphill.(p. 158)
  • Basement below grade but had windows, approx. 3 ft off floor, sitting on concrete foundation wall.(p. 158)
  • Couldn't recall window size or looking out them.(pp. 158-159)

Storage Tanks

  • Vertical storage tanks located outside main building.(pp. 159-160)
  • Recalled large cylinders (thousands of gallons), varying sizes, taller than wide.(p. 160)
  • Estimated 4-5 tanks.(p. 160)
  • Filled via pipes (from internal production or external truck deliveries).(pp. 160, 162-164)
  • Orientation: Back to Smith St -> tanks/building on left, river/tailrace on right.(p. 161)
  • Tanker trucks delivered via ports on outside (likely west) wall, accessed from driveway (right side of building).(pp. 163-164) Some deliveries used flexible hose to holding tank.(p. 164)

Site Layout (Using Exhibit 8 - Large Sanborn Map/Plan)

  • Reviewed Exhibit 8 (map from 2008 depo). Viewed Metro-Atlantic/NECC as one entity under Buonannos.(pp. 168-171)
  • Office: First building left off Rt 44 (Smith St), admin/secretary.(p. 170)
  • Process Area No. 1: Vague; maybe Joyal/Daley worked with vats.(p. 171)
  • Shipping: Truck deliveries (raw materials) / pickups (finished goods).(p. 172)
  • Connection: Liquid intake point(s) from trucks, along west wall.(pp. 172-173)
  • Storage tanks: Outside, along "back" (south?) wall, connected by catwalk.(pp. 173-174)
  • Reserve salt filter press: Inside basement near entry door; marked 2 press locations.(pp. 174-175) Later corrected a nearby line as the French drain path.(p. 176)
  • Process area No. 2: Initially basement work area (drum transfers, storage).(pp. 176-177) Later guessed label might refer to upper floor mixing/blending.(p. 177)
  • Dumpster / "40-S": Dumpster outside below large doors. "40-S" label = separate 40-S filter press in basement near reserve salt press.(pp. 177-179)
  • Driveway: Orange line west of main building.(p. 179)
  • Maintenance/Salt Drying Building: Separate building west of driveway.(p. 180)
  • Texas Tower: Location where hexachlorophene made.(p. 180)
  • Loading area (Orange): East of Texas Tower, NECC barrel handling.(p. 181)
  • Warehouse area (Orange Line): Inside building drum storage.(pp. 181-182)
  • Paint station (Arrow): Inside area for spray painting drums.(p. 182)
  • Drum removal / Burn pit / Burner: South area for reconditioning. Drums on track ("Pit"), through fire ("Burner"), then removed ("Drum removal").(pp. 183-185)
  • Wash station (Trapezoidal Bldg): For cleaning closed-head ("tight") drums with water/steam; drain beneath rack.(p. 186) Nadeau sandblasted nearby.(p. 187)
  • Drum storage: Area along river, drums stacked rows 3-5 high.(pp. 187-188)
  • "To dump": Arrow points to dump area at end of peninsula.(p. 188)

Waste Disposal (Filter Cake, Chipped Material, Dumpster Contents)

  • Empty raw material bags thrown into Dumpster.(p. 165)
  • Filter press cleanings might also go into Dumpster.(p. 166)
  • Solidified material chipped from vessels put in buckets. Based on prior depo (Exh 1, p. 43), likely put in barrel, possibly then Dumpster; uncertain now.(pp. 166-167)

Drum Reconditioning (Washing, Burning, Re-shaping)

  • Closed-head ("tight") drums cleaned internally at wash station (water/steam).(p. 186)
  • Other drums went through burn pit/burner to remove residue.(p. 184)
  • Post-cleaning/burning: inspected; if reusable, reshaped, painted, stored.(p. 189)
  • "Leaker" drums unfit for liquids sold as burn barrels.(p. 190)
  • Assumed some drums discarded in dump area; unsure.(p. 190)

French Drains & Discharges

  • Basement French drains ~1 ft wide, some grated.(pp. 164-165)
  • Confirmed prior trial testimony (Exhibit 4): basement wash water via French drains went "outside" to tailrace/river, not sewer.(pp. 191-193, reading pp. 199-201)
  • Regarding *seeing* discharge: Confirmed 2002 depo (Exh 1, p. 34) where seeing "cutout" called "assumption."(p. 192)
  • However, stated now:
    A. Yeah, I remember saying that but the more I think about it now, I do remember in fact it went out. I saw it go out.(p. 192)
  • Asserted discharge went onto land behind building, then to river; no sewer.(p. 193)

Examination by Ms. Baroni (NECC)

Examiner's Objective & Success Assessment

Ms. Baroni aimed to confirm the consistency of Mr. Nadeau's trial testimony regarding discharges going "outside" and to establish that the vast majority of his work occurred indoors. She achieved these objectives efficiently; the witness confirmed the trial testimony excerpts and agreed that most of his Metro-Atlantic work was inside.

Consistency of Prior Trial Testimony (French Drain Discharge)

  • Read trial testimony (Exhibit 4, p. 28-29) covering basement washdown, source (presses, carbon sludge), awareness of discharge to tailrace/river outside, not sewer.(pp. 199-200)
  • Nadeau confirmed giving that testimony and its consistency with current testimony.(p. 201)

Location of Work (Primarily Indoors)

  • Agreed most Metro-Atlantic work was indoors (main building: blending, tanks, filter press).(p. 201)
  • Smaller portion of time "across the street" (west) in salt drying building.(pp. 201-202)
  • Stated generally nothing to do outside, except brief checks of tanks.(p. 202)

Examination by Mr. Bryan (US Dept. of Air Force/Justice)

Examiner's Objective & Success Assessment

Mr. Bryan sought to firmly establish the timeline of employment, clarify the "interchangeable" employees concept, elicit more detail about the Texas Tower's appearance, confirm prior testimony about observing discolored/murky water in the tailrace, and explore the handling of plastic drum liners at NECC. He succeeded in confirming dates using prior testimony, clarifying that "interchangeable" primarily referred to maintenance staff, obtaining a physical description of the Texas Tower, confirming observations of murky water (while adding context of clear water days), and confirming the process for handling drum liners.

Employment Dates Confirmation

  • Reviewed Dec 17, 2002 depo (Exh 1, p. 29); agreed full-time Metro-Atlantic employment likely June '64 - Aug '65.(pp. 204-205)

Joint Operations/Ownership ("Interchangeable" Employees)

  • Reiterated belief families/ownership intertwined; lacked specifics.(p. 205)
  • Reviewed affidavit (Exh 3); confirmed statement some employees worked both facilities, were "interchangeable."(p. 206)
  • Clarified "interchangeable" primarily meant maintenance crew (e.g., Walt Murphy) working under union contract at either site as needed.(pp. 206-207)

Texas Tower Description

  • Described as relatively square building, possibly raised on pile-ons, steps on one side.(p. 207)
  • Estimated ~2 stories tall.(p. 207)
  • Never went inside. Didn't appear dilapidated; "just a building." Couldn't recall construction materials; prior plywood guess was based on cost.(p. 208)

French Drains & Tailrace Observations (Discoloration/Murkiness)

  • Reviewed Dec 17, 2002 depo (Exh 1, p. 35); confirmed testifying water color changed on floor, washed to drain/exit.(p. 209)
  • Confirmed prior testimony about observing tailrace become murky/change color at times (working at Metro-Atlantic).(p. 210)
  • Described tailrace conditions:
    A. There were times when the water was clear. You could see the fish and turtles in it. There were days when you could not do that, you could not see to the bottom of that. It was shallow water, it was not extremely deep. You could not see the bottom.(p. 211)
  • Couldn't recall seeing flow/ripples; seemed still but assumed connected to river. Hadn't walked down to confluence.(p. 213)

Drum Liners (Presence and Disposal at NECC)

  • Residue from cleaning "tight" drums ran onto/under rack at wash station; destination unknown.(pp. 211-212)
  • Confirmed prior testimony (trial, Exh 4, p. 14 re: NECC) some, not all, barrels had plastic liners/rubber gaskets.(pp. 214-215)
  • Recalled (refreshed): Liners sometimes pulled during unloading at NECC, dumped on ground, later collected into another drum; destination of that drum unknown.(p. 215)
  • Confirmed prior depo testimony stating bags/liners taken to "back part" (dump area) and dumped on ground.(p. 216)

Re-Examination by Mr. Pirozzolo (Emhart)

Examiner's Objective & Success Assessment

Mr. Pirozzolo aimed to establish Mr. Nadeau's lack of specific knowledge regarding dioxin, chemistry, and the business/property aspects of Metro-Atlantic/NECC, reinforcing his limited role as a laborer. He succeeded in eliciting testimony confirming these limitations.

Knowledge of Dioxin & Chemistry

  • Heard of dioxin generally, but not recognized as term/substance from employment time.(pp. 216-217)
  • Could not recall seeing any substance identified as containing dioxin.(p. 217)
  • Affirmed not a chemist, lacked chemistry understanding (basic hazard awareness only).(p. 217)
  • Wouldn't know if handled substances contained dioxin.(p. 217)

Role and Knowledge Limitations (Laborer, No Management/Accounting/Ownership Info)

  • Confirmed role was laborer.(p. 218)
  • Confirmed no involvement in accounting, management, boards.(p. 218)
  • Confirmed lacked knowledge of specific land ownership/boundaries.(p. 218)
  • These areas outside his duties/job responsibilities.(p. 219)
